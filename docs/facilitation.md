# Facilitation Guide

How to run an effective retrospective with RetroBoard Pro. This guide covers each phase, facilitator tools, and tips for keeping your team engaged.

## Before the Meeting

1. **Create a sprint** for the current iteration (if not already done)
2. **Create a board** — pick a template that fits your team's mood:
   - New team or first retro? → **Start / Stop / Continue** (simple, actionable)
   - Team in a rut? → **Mad / Sad / Glad** (surfaces emotions)
   - Deep reflection needed? → **4Ls** (comprehensive)
   - Want something visual? → **Sailboat** (metaphor-driven)
3. **Share the board link** with your team before the meeting starts

## Phase 1: Icebreaker (2-3 minutes)

The board opens with a random warmup question from one of 5 categories: Fun, Team-Building, Reflective, Creative, or Quick.

**What the facilitator does:**
- Let the question display — team members respond via reactions or short answers
- When energy feels right, click "Start Writing" to move to the next phase

**Why it matters:** Teams that skip warmup tend to write fewer and less honest cards. 2 minutes of icebreaker pays for itself in retro quality.

**Tip:** If your team always picks "skip," try the Creative or Fun categories. Reflective questions work better for established teams.

## Phase 2: Write (5-8 minutes)

Team members add cards to columns. In anonymous mode, authors are hidden until reveal.

**What the facilitator does:**
- **Start a timer** (5 minutes is a good default for teams of 4-8)
- **Don't talk.** Silence during writing prevents anchoring bias
- Let the timer run out — resist ending early even if cards seem done

**What team members do:**
- Write one thought per card (short, specific)
- Add cards to whichever column fits
- No discussion yet — just capture everything

**Tip:** If your team writes too few cards, try anonymous mode. People write more honestly when their name isn't attached.

## Phase 3: Group (3-5 minutes)

The facilitator clusters related cards into themes.

**What the facilitator does:**
- Read through all cards quickly
- Drag related cards into groups
- Name each group with a short theme title
- Ask "Did I miss any connections?" before moving on

**What team members do:**
- Watch and suggest groupings
- Point out cards that seem related

**Tip:** Don't over-group. 4-6 groups is ideal. If every card is in its own group, the voting phase becomes meaningless.

## Phase 4: Vote (2-3 minutes)

Team votes on which cards/groups matter most. Each member gets a limited number of votes (configurable per board).

**What the facilitator does:**
- **Start a timer** (2 minutes is enough)
- Remind the team: "Vote on what you want to _discuss_, not just what you agree with"
- **Lock the board** after voting ends if you want to prevent late votes

**What team members do:**
- Distribute votes across cards that deserve discussion time
- Don't dump all votes on one card (unless it really is the top priority)

**Tip:** 3-5 votes per person works well for teams of 4-8. Too many votes dilutes the signal.

## Phase 5: Discuss (10-15 minutes)

Walk through the top-voted items. This is where the real value happens.

**What the facilitator does:**
- **Use focus mode** — spotlight one card/group at a time
- Start with the highest-voted item
- Time-box each discussion (2-3 minutes per item)
- Ask: "What can we _do_ about this?" to steer toward action
- Move focus to the next item when discussion stalls

**What team members do:**
- Share context on why this matters
- Suggest concrete improvements (not vague wishes)
- Listen — don't repeat what's already been said

**Tip:** You won't discuss everything. That's fine. The voting already prioritized. Cover the top 3-5 items well rather than rushing through all of them.

## Phase 6: Action (3-5 minutes)

Convert discussion into assigned action items with due dates.

**What the facilitator does:**
- For each discussed item, ask: "What's the action? Who owns it? When is it due?"
- Create action items with assignees and due dates
- Carry over unfinished items from the previous sprint (one-click carry-over)
- Review the final list — every item should have an owner

**What team members do:**
- Volunteer for action items in their area
- Push back on vague actions ("improve testing" → "add integration tests for auth flow by Friday")

**Tip:** If an action item doesn't have an owner, it won't get done. Assign everything.

## Facilitator Tools Reference

| Tool | When to use | Access |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Timer** | Write, Vote, and Discuss phases | Toolbar → Timer icon |
| **Lock board** | After voting, during discussion | Toolbar → Lock icon |
| **Reveal cards** | After write phase in anonymous mode | Toolbar → Reveal |
| **Focus mode** | During discuss phase | Click any card/group |
| **Phase controls** | To advance, go back, or restart | Phase bar at top |

## After the Meeting

1. **Export the board** — send a Markdown or HTML summary to the team channel
2. **Check analytics** — are health scores trending up? Is participation balanced?
3. **Follow up on action items** — check them off as they're completed
4. **Carry over** unfinished items to the next retro automatically

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Same person talks the whole time | Use focus mode + timer per item |
| Cards are too vague ("things are bad") | Add prompt text to columns, model specific cards yourself |
| Team skips action items | Don't end the meeting without at least 2 assigned actions |
| Retros feel repetitive | Rotate templates. Try Sailboat after 3 rounds of Start/Stop/Continue. |
| Low participation | Turn on anonymous mode. Or try the icebreaker — warmup increases engagement. |
| Action items never get done | Review last sprint's actions at the START of each retro. Carry over incomplete ones. |
